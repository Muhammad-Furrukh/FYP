\chapter{Methodology}
\label{Chapter5}

Distribute the project goals into smaller objectives/modules and outline deliverables for each objective. Explain the modules of the project through a system-level block diagram. Students may also mention tools, technologies, and suitability of the method(s) to be employed with justification. In case of a research problem, outline the approaches that will be investigated in the project. The proposed work must have a significant design element, open-ended problems are strictly discouraged. The proposed work must also tend to one or more real-world applications. ($4-6$ pages)

Further breakdown of this chapter for the guidance of students is given below.

\section{Design Constraints}
\label{sec:DC}

The design constraints must be clearly listed. Care must be taken to ensure that the listed constraints are practical and achievable.

\section{Technical Approach}
\label{sec:TA}

Describe the technical strategy to achieve your objectives. Outline the main project phases and mention specific components, software tools, and simulation techniques you plan to use.

\section{Block Diagram}
\label{sec:BD}

Insert a clear and well-labeled block diagram here. The diagram should visually represent the main functional blocks of your system and the signal/power flow between them.

\section{Flow Chart}
\label{sec:FC}

Insert a flow chart that illustrates the operational logic of your project's software or control system. Use standard symbols to show the process from start to finish, including initialization, data reading, decision points, and actions.

\section{Applications}
\label{sec:app}

Write a comprehensive subsection on the application of the proposed work for solving one or more real-world problems.
